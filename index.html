About

I am a doctoral candidate in Texts and Technology at the University of Central Florida. My work sits at the intersection of fan studies and digital communication and cultures. My current research explores how fan communities perceive, remix, and negotiate meaning of adaptations featuring transgenerational fictional characters like Superman and Spider-Man—and how those perceptions speak to broader questions of authorship, identity, and industry practices.

Alongside my scholarship, I am a dedicated, learning-centered educator. I have taught for eight semesters across UCF's Department of English and Games and Interactive Media, including DIG3024 Introduction to Digital Cultures and Narratives and ENC4415 Digital Rhetorics and the Modern Dialectic.

My courses are built around learning strategies that support students at different stages and skill levels. I use formative and summative assessment to track progress and identify where students need help, and I treat inclusion as a deliberate part of curriculum design so that the diversity of my students strengthens the learning environment. My research keeps me current in the field and grounds my pedagogy in the scholarship of teaching and learning.
